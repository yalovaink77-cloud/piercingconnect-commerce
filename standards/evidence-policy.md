# Evidence Policy

Version: 1.0
Status: Active
Project: PiercingConnect Commerce Blueprint

---

## Purpose

This policy defines how PiercingConnect evaluates evidence, safety information, product claims, and professional guidance.

The goal is to protect user trust and prevent unsafe, exaggerated, or commercially biased recommendations.

---

## Core Rule

Safety claims require evidence.

Commercial claims require transparency.

Medical claims require caution.

---

## Evidence Priority

PiercingConnect should prioritize evidence in this order:

1. Recognized medical and public health sources
2. Professional piercing standards and associations
3. Peer-reviewed research
4. Manufacturer documentation
5. Professional piercer consensus
6. Real-world user experience
7. Affiliate or marketplace descriptions

Affiliate descriptions must never be treated as primary evidence.

---

## High-Risk Topics

The following topics require extra caution:

* infection
* allergic reaction
* embedded jewelry
* rejection
* migration
* severe swelling
* excessive bleeding
* pus or unusual discharge
* fever
* severe pain
* oral piercing complications
* genital piercing complications
* pregnancy-related piercing questions
* child or minor piercing questions
* medical conditions affecting healing

For these topics, content must avoid diagnosis and encourage professional evaluation when appropriate.

---

## Diagnosis Rule

PiercingConnect does not diagnose medical conditions.

Allowed wording:

* "may be a sign of"
* "can sometimes indicate"
* "could be related to"
* "consider contacting a qualified professional"

Avoid wording:

* "this is definitely infected"
* "this product will cure it"
* "you do not need a doctor"
* "ignore it"
* "guaranteed treatment"

---

## Product Claim Rule

Products may support aftercare.

Products must not be described as curing infections, eliminating medical conditions, or replacing professional care.

Allowed wording:

* "may help keep the area clean"
* "can support a simple aftercare routine"
* "commonly used for gentle rinsing"
* "designed for wound cleansing"

Avoid wording:

* "cures piercing bumps"
* "removes infection"
* "heals any piercing fast"
* "guaranteed results"

---

## Affiliate Separation Rule

Affiliate opportunity must be evaluated separately from safety and quality.

A product may have strong affiliate potential but still be excluded if it fails safety, evidence, or trust standards.

Affiliate revenue must never determine medical recommendations.

---

## Source Notes

Important safety or product records should include source notes when possible.

Source notes may include:

* official product documentation
* ingredient lists
* professional association guidance
* wound care references
* regulatory information
* manufacturer safety information
* expert review notes

---

## Review Status

Each major knowledge asset should eventually have one of the following statuses:

* draft
* needs review
* reviewed
* active
* outdated
* deprecated

High-risk topics should not be marked active until reviewed.

---

## Last Reviewed Date

Structured data records should include a `last_reviewed` field when possible.

Example:

```yaml
last_reviewed: 2026-07-08
review_status: active
```

---

## Medical Referral Rule

Content should recommend professional evaluation when symptoms may indicate serious risk.

Examples:

* worsening pain
* spreading redness
* heat
* fever
* significant swelling
* green or foul-smelling discharge
* jewelry embedding
* difficulty breathing or swallowing
* severe bleeding

PiercingConnect should never discourage medical care.

---

## Unsafe Advice Rule

PiercingConnect should avoid recommending:

* alcohol
* hydrogen peroxide
* harsh antiseptics
* tea tree oil on fresh piercings
* homemade salt mixtures as a preferred option
* twisting jewelry
* removing jewelry during suspected infection without professional guidance
* aggressive cleaning
* over-cleaning
* unverified home remedies

These topics may be discussed educationally, but not recommended as standard aftercare.

---

## Evidence Gap Rule

If evidence is incomplete, say so clearly.

Allowed wording:

* "Evidence is limited."
* "Professional recommendations vary."
* "This should be treated cautiously."
* "Availability and guidance may differ by region."

Uncertainty should be disclosed rather than hidden.

---

## Final Principle

PiercingConnect earns trust by being careful, transparent, and safety-first.

No article, product page, or affiliate recommendation should violate this policy.
