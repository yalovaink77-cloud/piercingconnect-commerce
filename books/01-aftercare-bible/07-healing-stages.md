# Purpose

This document provides human-readable profiles for piercing healing stages stored in the structured data layer.

The structured data (`data/healing-stages/*.yaml`) is the single source of truth.

**Important:** Healing timelines vary by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare. PiercingConnect does not guarantee fixed healing durations. Follow `standards/evidence-policy.md`. No medical diagnosis.

---

# Healing Timeline Overview

```text
Fresh Piercing → Early Healing → Active Healing → Late Healing → Mature Piercing
                                      ↕
                            Irritated Piercing (may occur at any stage)
                                      ↓
                            Retired Piercing (removed, rejected, or abandoned)
```

Linear stages are editorial models — real healing is not always sequential or predictable.

# Active Healing

**Data Source**

`data/healing-stages/active-healing.yaml`

**Research**

`research/healing-stages/active-healing_research.md`

## Summary

A mid-healing phase when the channel may still be forming but daily symptoms are often less acute. Progress depends on piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and consistent aftercare.

## Typical Timeframe

May last from several weeks to many months depending on piercing type and individual factors. There is no guaranteed end date for any piercing category.

*Healing varies by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare. No guaranteed timelines.*

## Cleaning Guidance

- Maintain a sustainable saline or shower-rinse routine per piercer guidance
- Balance cleaning with avoiding over-cleaning that may dry or irritate tissue
- Address moisture trapping from hair products, tight clothing, or occlusive dressings

## When to Seek Help

- Healing that appears stalled beyond your piercer's expected range for your piercing
- New bumps, migration signs, or allergic-type reactions
- Uncertainty about jewelry change readiness

# Early Healing

**Data Source**

`data/healing-stages/early-healing.yaml`

**Research**

`research/healing-stages/early-healing_research.md`

## Summary

The period when initial acute symptoms may begin to settle while the piercing channel is still fragile. Duration varies by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare.

## Typical Timeframe

Commonly spans several weeks for simpler piercings and may extend into months for cartilage or body piercings. Your piercer can help set realistic expectations for your specific piercing.

*Healing varies by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare. No guaranteed timelines.*

## Cleaning Guidance

- Continue gentle sterile saline rinsing as directed; once or twice daily is common for body piercings
- Remove obvious debris and dried discharge gently without picking aggressively
- Avoid introducing new harsh products or home remedies without piercer input

## When to Seek Help

- Irritation bumps or symptoms that do not improve after removing mechanical causes
- Suspected migration, rejection, or embedding changes
- Signs that may warrant medical evaluation alongside piercer assessment

# Fresh Piercing

**Data Source**

`data/healing-stages/fresh-piercing.yaml`

**Research**

`research/healing-stages/fresh-piercing_research.md`

## Summary

The immediate post-piercing period when the site is an open wound and initial swelling, tenderness, and minor bleeding may occur. Healing duration varies widely by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare.

## Typical Timeframe

Often the first several days to roughly two weeks for many piercings, but this varies widely by location and individual factors. Confirm expectations with your piercer rather than relying on fixed schedules.

*Healing varies by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare. No guaranteed timelines.*

## Cleaning Guidance

- Spray or rinse with sterile saline labeled for wound wash once or twice daily per piercer guidance
- Avoid alcohol, hydrogen peroxide, harsh soaps, and ointments on the open wound
- Wash hands before touching the area or jewelry

## When to Seek Help

- Signs that may suggest infection such as spreading redness, fever, or foul discharge
- Jewelry that appears embedded or too tight to clean around
- Bleeding that is heavy or does not slow with gentle care

# Irritated Piercing

**Data Source**

`data/healing-stages/irritated-piercing.yaml`

**Research**

`research/healing-stages/irritated-piercing_research.md`

## Summary

A state of heightened local irritation that may occur at any point in the healing timeline when mechanical, chemical, or jewelry-related triggers are present. Not a diagnosis.

## Typical Timeframe

May last days to weeks depending on trigger removal and piercing context. No fixed recovery timeline applies.

*Healing varies by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare. No guaranteed timelines.*

## Cleaning Guidance

- Return to simple sterile saline rinsing once or twice daily if piercer agrees
- Stop alcohol, hydrogen peroxide, tea tree oil, and harsh soaps immediately
- Reduce cleaning frequency if over-cleaning contributed to dryness or irritation

## When to Seek Help

- Irritation that does not improve after removing likely triggers within a reasonable period
- Signs that may suggest infection requiring medical evaluation
- Suspected migration, rejection, or embedding

# Late Healing

**Data Source**

`data/healing-stages/late-healing.yaml`

**Research**

`research/healing-stages/late-healing_research.md`

## Summary

A later phase when the channel may be approaching maturity for some piercings while still susceptible to setbacks. Timelines vary widely and cannot be guaranteed.

## Typical Timeframe

May extend from several months to a year or longer for some piercing types. Confirm readiness for jewelry changes or reduced aftercare with your piercer.

*Healing varies by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare. No guaranteed timelines.*

## Cleaning Guidance

- Saline rinsing may be reduced in frequency only with piercer guidance
- Resume gentle cleaning after sports, swimming, or known contamination exposure
- Avoid harsh products even when the piercing feels mostly healed

## When to Seek Help

- New migration, rejection, or embedding signs in late healing
- Persistent bumps that return after irritation sources are removed
- Pain or discharge returning after a period of stability

# Mature Piercing

**Data Source**

`data/healing-stages/mature-piercing.yaml`

**Research**

`research/healing-stages/mature-piercing_research.md`

## Summary

A stabilized piercing channel that may tolerate routine jewelry care for many people, though individual sensitivity can persist. Maturity timing varies by piercing type and cannot be guaranteed on a fixed schedule.

## Typical Timeframe

May occur months to years after piercing depending on type, anatomy, and care history. Piercer confirmation is preferable before assuming full maturity.

*Healing varies by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare. No guaranteed timelines.*

## Cleaning Guidance

- Routine saline rinse may be used after contamination or irritation flares
- Normal shower rinsing may be sufficient for many mature piercings day to day
- Avoid reintroducing harsh antiseptics for routine maintenance

## When to Seek Help

- New persistent symptoms in a previously stable piercing
- Difficulty reinserting jewelry after removal
- Signs that may suggest infection after new trauma

# Retired Piercing

**Data Source**

`data/healing-stages/retired-piercing.yaml`

**Research**

`research/healing-stages/retired-piercing_research.md`

## Summary

A piercing that has been intentionally abandoned or removed, or that rejected/migrated to closure. Aftercare focuses on wound hygiene and scar care, not channel maintenance.

## Typical Timeframe

Channel closure may begin within hours to days for some piercings when jewelry is removed; full skin remodeling may take weeks to months. Individual variation is significant.

*Healing varies by piercing type, anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare. No guaranteed timelines.*

## Cleaning Guidance

- Keep the area clean with gentle shower rinsing unless a clinician directs otherwise
- Avoid harsh antiseptics on closing tissue unless medically indicated
- Do not pick at scabs or closing channel openings

## When to Seek Help

- Signs that may suggest abscess, severe infection, or retained foreign material
- Bleeding or pain disproportionate to expected closure
- Piercer or medical guidance before re-piercing the same site
