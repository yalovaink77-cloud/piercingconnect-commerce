# Piercing Types

Version: 1.0

Status: Active

---

# Purpose

This document provides human-readable profiles for piercing types stored in the structured data layer.

The structured data (`data/piercing-types/*.yaml`) is the single source of truth.

**Important:** Healing times are expressed as ranges where supported by professional guidance — not guarantees. Follow `standards/evidence-policy.md`. No medical diagnosis.

APP guidance (Piercing FAQ) notes that tougher tissue such as ear cartilage, navels, and nostrils may take **six months or longer**, while softer tissues such as earlobes may heal more quickly. Individual variation is significant.

# Anti-Tragus

**Data Source**

`data/piercing-types/anti-tragus.yaml`

**Research**

`research/piercing-types/anti-tragus_research.md`

## Summary

Anti-Tragus piercing placed through ear cartilage.  Healing varies by anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare.

## Healing Time

Ear cartilage may take six months or longer to heal per APP comparative guidance for tougher tissue. Individual timelines vary widely.

*Not a guaranteed timeline. Healing varies by anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare.*

## Aftercare Notes

- Spray sterile saline labeled for wound wash once or twice daily per APP body aftercare
- Avoid sleeping on the piercing; use travel pillow or pressure protection if needed
- Do not rotate or move jewelry during cleaning

# Labret

**Data Source**

`data/piercing-types/labret.yaml`

**Research**

`research/piercing-types/labret_research.md`

## Summary

Labret piercing through the lower lip, typically below the vermillion border. Oral-adjacent aftercare applies; healing varies.

## Healing Time

Oral and lip piercings involve mucosal tissue that may heal on a different schedule than external skin. Timelines vary; follow piercer oral aftercare guidance.

*Not a guaranteed timeline. Healing varies by anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare.*

## Aftercare Notes

- Use alcohol-free antiseptic mouthwash 4–5 times daily for oral piercings per APP oral guidance
- Rinse after meals and before bed; use soft-bristled toothbrush carefully around jewelry
- Avoid oral contact and unprotected sexual or oral activity during early healing per piercer guidance

**Research**

`research/piercing-types/navel_research.md`

## Summary

Navel piercing through the rim of the navel when anatomy provides suitable tissue. APP notes navel among tougher tissue requiring six months or longer.

## Healing Time

Navel piercings are among tougher tissue types that may take six months or longer per APP guidance. Snagging and moisture commonly affect progress.

*Not a guaranteed timeline. Healing varies by anatomy, jewelry, health, and aftercare.*

## Aftercare Notes

- Sterile saline rinse once or twice daily; keep area dry between cleanings
- Wear low-rise or breathable clothing; avoid waistband pressure and snagging
- Avoid submerging in baths, hot tubs, or untreated water during early healing
