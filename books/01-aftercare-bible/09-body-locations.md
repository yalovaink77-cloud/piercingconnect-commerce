# Ear

**Data Source**

`data/body-locations/ear.yaml`

**Research**

`research/body-locations/ear_research.md`

## Summary

The ear region includes soft lobe tissue and avascular cartilage structures. Piercing types and healing timelines vary widely between lobe and cartilage placements.

## Anatomy Notes

The ear combines soft earlobe tissue with cartilage structures including the helix, concha, tragus, and anti-tragus. Cartilage has limited direct blood supply compared with soft tissue, which may contribute to slower healing for cartilage piercings per APP comparative guidance.

## Piercing Types

`helix`, `forward-helix`, `flat`, `conch`, `tragus`, `anti-tragus`, `rook`, `daith`, `industrial`, `lobe`, `upper-lobe`

## Healing Characteristics

- Soft earlobe tissue may heal faster than cartilage per APP comparative guidance
- Ear cartilage, navels, and nostrils are grouped as tougher tissue that may take six months or longer
- Cartilage piercings may be prone to irritation bumps when slept on or snagged

## Aftercare Notes

- Spray sterile saline labeled for wound wash once or twice daily per APP body aftercare
- Avoid sleeping on cartilage piercings; use pressure protection if needed
- Do not rotate jewelry during cleaning

## Related Problems

`sleeping-irritation`, `irritation-bump`, `migration`, `delayed-healing`, `snagging`, `swelling`

# Face

**Data Source**

`data/body-locations/face.yaml`

**Research**

`research/body-locations/face_research.md`

## Summary

Facial piercing region in this taxonomy covers periorbital and nasal bridge surface-adjacent placements such as eyebrow and bridge piercings. Migration and snagging are common concerns.

## Anatomy Notes

Facial piercings in this record include eyebrow tissue and the nasal bridge surface. These are not typically through thick muscle but involve surface or shallow tissue planes with varying migration risk.

## Piercing Types

`bridge`, `eyebrow`

## Healing Characteristics

- Surface-adjacent facial piercings may have higher migration and rejection risk per APP troubleshooting
- Healing may take six months or longer for tougher tissue categories
- Exposure to hair products, glasses, and environmental contaminants may increase irritation risk

## Aftercare Notes

- Sterile saline rinsing per APP body aftercare guidance
- Protect from snagging by hair, glasses, towels, and clothing
- Monitor for migration signs — consult piercer promptly if jewelry position changes

## Related Problems

`migration`, `rejection`, `snagging`, `irritation-bump`, `delayed-healing`, `infection-concern`

# Lip

**Data Source**

`data/body-locations/lip.yaml`

**Research**

`research/body-locations/lip_research.md`

## Summary

The lip region includes labret, philtrum, medusa, monroe, and vertical labret placements through lip tissue. Oral-adjacent hygiene applies alongside external saline care.

## Anatomy Notes

Lip piercings pass through lip tissue and may have both external skin and intraoral exposure depending on placement. Vertical labret may avoid intraoral entry. Tissue is vascular and mobile, affecting swelling and jewelry fit.

## Piercing Types

`labret`, `philtrum`, `medusa`, `monroe`, `vertical-labret`

## Healing Characteristics

- Lip and oral-adjacent tissue may heal on a different schedule than external skin piercings
- Initial swelling may require longer initial jewelry; downsizing timing varies per APP oral guidance
- Excessively long oral-adjacent jewelry may contribute to teeth or gum contact per APP FAQ

## Aftercare Notes

- Use alcohol-free antiseptic mouthwash 4–5 times daily for oral exposure per APP oral aftercare
- Sterile saline for external skin side per APP body aftercare
- Avoid oral contact and unprotected activity during early healing per piercer guidance

## Related Problems

`swelling`, `crusting`, `moisture-irritation`, `overcleaning`, `infection-concern`, `pressure-irritation`

# Navel

**Data Source**

`data/body-locations/navel.yaml`

**Research**

`research/body-locations/navel_research.md`

## Summary

The navel region includes navel rim piercings when anatomy provides suitable tissue. APP groups navels with tougher tissue that may take six months or longer; snagging and moisture are common concerns.

## Anatomy Notes

Navel piercings typically pass through the upper or lower navel rim rather than the deep navel cavity. Not all navel anatomy is suitable. APP FAQ addresses pregnancy and jewelry retention for healed navels.

## Piercing Types

`navel`

## Healing Characteristics

- APP notes navel among tougher tissue that may take six months or longer
- Waistbands, clothing friction, and moisture trapping commonly affect healing
- Migration and rejection may occur if tissue depth or jewelry fit is inadequate

## Aftercare Notes

- Sterile saline rinse once or twice daily; keep area dry between cleanings
- Wear low-rise or breathable clothing; avoid waistband pressure
- Avoid submerging in baths, hot tubs, or untreated water during early healing

## Related Problems

`migration`, `rejection`, `snagging`, `moisture-irritation`, `irritation-bump`, `delayed-healing`

# Nipple

**Data Source**

`data/body-locations/nipple.yaml`

**Research**

`research/body-locations/nipple_research.md`

## Summary

The nipple region includes nipple piercings through nipple tissue when anatomically suitable. Extended healing is common; APP addresses pregnancy, breastfeeding, and jewelry considerations.

## Anatomy Notes

Nipple piercings pass through nipple tissue. APP FAQ states milk ducts are numerous pore-like structures and breastfeeding is generally not prevented by usual piercing sizes, but jewelry should be removed during feeding. Pregnancy may affect healing and existing piercings.

## Piercing Types

`nipple`

## Healing Characteristics

- Nipple piercings often require many months of healing; individual timelines vary
- Compression from bras, sports, and bags may cause pressure irritation
- APP notes pregnancy and immune changes may affect healing — piercing during pregnancy is discouraged

## Aftercare Notes

- Sterile saline rinsing per APP body aftercare guidance
- Wear breathable, non-occlusive clothing when possible
- Avoid trauma from sports, seatbelts, and sleeping pressure

## Related Problems

`pressure-irritation`, `delayed-healing`, `irritation-bump`, `infection-concern`, `migration`, `snagging`

# Nose

**Data Source**

`data/body-locations/nose.yaml`

**Research**

`research/body-locations/nose_research.md`

## Summary

The nose region includes nostril, high nostril, and septum piercing placements through soft tissue and cartilage. APP groups nostrils with tougher tissue requiring extended healing.

## Anatomy Notes

Nostril piercings pass through nostril soft tissue and cartilage at the rim or wall. Septum piercings target the sweet spot of thin soft tissue below nasal cartilage when anatomy permits. Placement depth and angle require professional assessment.

## Piercing Types

`nostril`, `high-nostril`, `septum`

## Healing Characteristics

- APP notes tougher tissue such as nostrils may take six months or longer to heal
- Facial piercings may be more exposed to airborne contaminants than clothed body areas per APP commentary
- Septum healing timelines vary based on sweet-spot anatomy and individual factors

## Aftercare Notes

- Sterile saline wound wash once or twice daily per APP body aftercare
- Avoid makeup, skincare, and unnecessary touching on nostril piercings
- Do not flip septum jewelry before piercer confirms readiness

## Related Problems

`irritation-bump`, `swelling`, `crusting`, `delayed-healing`, `infection-concern`, `jewelry-quality-issue`

# Oral

**Data Source**

`data/body-locations/oral.yaml`

**Research**

`research/body-locations/oral_research.md`

## Summary

The oral region includes intraoral piercings such as tongue and smiley (frenulum). APP oral aftercare guidance emphasizes alcohol-free mouthwash and downsizing to protect teeth and gums.

## Anatomy Notes

Oral piercings pass through mucosal tissue including tongue muscle and frenulum. High vascularity may affect swelling. Anatomical suitability varies — especially for smiley frenulum piercings.

## Piercing Types

`tongue`, `smiley`

## Healing Characteristics

- Mucosal tissue may heal on a different timeline than external skin
- Significant initial tongue swelling is commonly reported; individual variation is significant
- APP notes excessively long oral jewelry can damage teeth and gums until downsized

## Aftercare Notes

- Rinse with alcohol-free antiseptic mouthwash 4–5 times daily per APP oral aftercare
- Use soft-bristled toothbrush carefully around jewelry
- Avoid spicy, acidic, or irritating foods if they increase discomfort — piercer guidance varies

## Related Problems

`swelling`, `crusting`, `moisture-irritation`, `overcleaning`, `infection-concern`, `migration`

# Surface

**Data Source**

`data/body-locations/surface.yaml`

**Research**

`research/body-locations/surface_research.md`

## Summary

Surface piercing category describes piercings that travel under skin rather than through a distinct entry-exit tissue column, or piercings with elevated migration and rejection risk. Includes conceptual guidance for surface-adjacent placements.

## Anatomy Notes

Surface piercings rely on a shallow channel under the skin with limited tissue depth between entry and exit. APP FAQ discusses surface anchors as body piercings with long-term temporary lifespan and rejection risk. Bridge and eyebrow piercings share surface-adjacent behavior.

## Piercing Types

`bridge`, `eyebrow`

## Healing Characteristics

- APP notes surface anchors may be rejected by the body over months to years
- Tissue between entry and exit may narrow during migration per APP troubleshooting
- Surface piercings may require more vigilant monitoring than standard through-piercings

## Aftercare Notes

- Minimize mechanical trauma, snagging, and pressure on surface channels
- Sterile saline rinsing per APP guidance
- Consult piercer immediately if tissue between openings narrows or jewelry moves toward surface

## Related Problems

`migration`, `rejection`, `snagging`, `irritation-bump`, `delayed-healing`, `embedded-jewelry`
